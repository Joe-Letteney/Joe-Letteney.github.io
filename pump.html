Rotary Vane Pump

CNC Milling • Sand Casting • Laser Cutter • Water Jetter • Lathe • Sheet Metal Bending

Project Overview

The objective of this project was to manufacture and assemble a fully functional rotary vane pump from individual components. All parts were produced in-house using a wide range of manufacturing processes, requiring careful planning, precise execution, and iterative problem-solving throughout the build.

The fabrication process incorporated the following manufacturing techniques:

  • CNC and manual milling
  • Manual lathe operations
  • Sand casting
  • Laser cutting
  • Water jet cutting
  • Sheet metal bending
  • Sweat fitting and assembly

This hands-on experience provided a comprehensive understanding of the practical constraints associated with each process, including achievable tolerances, surface finish limitations, setup time, and manufacturability considerations. Several components were intentionally designed with suboptimal features, requiring critical evaluation and adaptive machining strategies. As a result, the project emphasized the importance of designing parts with manufacturing efficiency, accuracy, and assembly in mind.

Step 1: Manufacturing

The primary focus of this phase was the fabrication of the individual pump components. This required the application of multiple machining techniques while maintaining strict dimensional accuracy and repeatability. Throughout the manufacturing process, careful attention was given to tolerance stack-ups, fit classifications, and precision measurement using calipers, micrometers, and dial indicators. These considerations were essential to ensuring that all components interfaced correctly during final assembly and functioned as intended.

Step 2: Troubleshooting

During assembly, several mechanical challenges were identified, including a slightly oversized rotor, burr formation within the vane slots, and a minor misalignment in the bearing housing. Resolving these issues required iterative disassembly, corrective machining, and incremental fitting adjustments. This phase reinforced the importance of attention to detail, tolerance control, and systematic troubleshooting when transitioning from individual parts to a fully operational mechanical system.
