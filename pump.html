Rotary Vane Pump

CNC Milling • Sand Casting • Laser Cutter • Water Jetter • Lathe • Sheet Metal Bending

Project Overview

The goal of this project was to machine all the parts of a working rotary vane pump. This required a wide variety of machining processes including:

  • CNC/Manual Milling
  • Manual Lathe
  • Sand Casting
  • Laser Cutter
  • Water Jetter
  • Sheet Metal Bending
  • Sweat Fitting

This hands on experience with each of these manufacturing processes gave me a much deeper understanding of the time requirements, tolerances, and limitations of each process. This process also gave me experience with thinking about how to design for manufacturing as many of the parts in the pump that we were building were intentionally poorly designed.

Step 1: Manufacturing

The primary focus of the project was machining the individual components of the pump. This phase provided in-depth experience with a variety of machining operations and key considerations such as tolerance stack-ups, component fit types, and precision measurement techniques. These skills were critical in ensuring all parts met the required specifications.

Step 2: Troubleshooting

During assembly, several challenges were encountered, including a slightly oversized rotor, minor burrs on vane slots, and a bearing housing that was slightly misaligned. Addressing these issues required iterative machining and adjustment to achieve precise fits and ensure smooth operation. This phase reinforced problem-solving skills and attention to detail in a practical engineering context
