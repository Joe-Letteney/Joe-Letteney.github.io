Gasoline Fuel Pump

Mechanical Design • Design For Manufacturing • GD&T • Project Documentation

Project Overview

This project extended previous rotary vane pump manufacturing experience by addressing a specific application while resolving identified manufacturing constraints. The objective was to design a gasoline fuel pump suitable for heavy-duty truck applications, with particular emphasis on cost-effective production and ease of assembly. The project deliverables included a comprehensive drawing package with detailed GD&T specifications for all machined components, along with complete assembly documentation.

Phase 1: Material Selection

Material selection was driven by operational requirements of high-pressure performance and large-scale production economics. The selection process prioritized materials demonstrating gasoline compatibility, high tensile strength, and cost efficiency. Through systematic analysis using Granta EduPack, gray cast iron was selected for the main housing, while AISI 3031 alloy steel was specified for the rotor and shaft assembly. These materials were chosen for their complementary properties, including minimal thermal expansion coefficients critical for maintaining tight tolerances during operation.

Phase 2: Engineering Analysis

Comprehensive engineering calculations were performed to ensure operational viability within specified constraints. Key analyses included PV value determination at the vane tip to inform material selection, finite element analysis of the housing to verify pressure containment capability, wear rate calculations to validate service life requirements, and bolt clamping force analysis for assembly integrity. These foundational calculations directly informed subsequent design decisions and established performance baselines for the pump assembly. Phase 3: Design Challenges and Solutions

Analysis revealed an exceptionally high PV value at the vane tip, necessitating elimination of all polymer options in favor of hardened tool steel. To mitigate the resulting high outward forces on the vanes, an innovative vane geometry was developed that permits fluid circulation while maintaining sealing integrity. This design achieves near-perfect pressure balance across the vane surfaces, with centrifugal force becoming the dominant outward load component. The steel-on-steel interface between vanes and housing presented significant wear concerns, which were addressed through the integration of precision-machined PEEK inserts. These inserts substantially reduce the wear rate, enabling the pump to achieve its specified service life.

Phase 4: Design for Manufacturing

Manufacturing cost optimization for high-volume production was achieved through strategic process selection. The main housing was designed for sand casting with a single-core configuration, significantly reducing tooling complexity and per-unit cost. Concurrently, the PEEK insert geometry was optimized for injection molding, ensuring consistent quality and dimensional accuracy while maintaining manufacturing efficiency at scale.

Phase 5: Technical Documentation

The project culminated in the development of a complete technical drawing package. This documentation suite includes detailed part drawings with comprehensive GD&T specifications, casting drawings for all cast components with complete pattern and core specifications, and injection molding drawings for polymer inserts with full process parameters. The drawing set provides manufacturing-ready documentation suitable for immediate production implementation.
