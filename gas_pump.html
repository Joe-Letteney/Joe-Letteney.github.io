Gasoline Fuel Pump

Mechanical Design • Design for Manufacturing • GD&T • Project Documentation

Project Overview

This project builds on prior experience designing and manufacturing a rotary vane pump. The objective was to redesign the pump for a specific automotive application while addressing performance, durability, and manufacturability limitations identified in earlier designs.

The system was developed to meet the following specifications:

  • Working fluid: Gasoline for a fuel injection system
  • Operating temperature: −40°F to 140°F
  • Inlet pressure: 4 bar
  • Outlet pressure: 300 bar (4351 psi)
  • Operating speed: 2000 rpm
  • Service life: 200,000 miles (MTBF)
  • Application: Full-size pickup truck
  • Annual production volume: 2,000,000 units

Phase 1: Engineering Analysis

Detailed engineering analysis was performed to determine the forces, wear mechanisms, and operating limits of each component. This included PV calculations at the vane tips, wear rate estimation, pressure loading on structural components, bolt clamping analysis, tolerance stack-up evaluation, and thermal expansion effects. These analyses established the design requirements used throughout development.

Phase 2: Material Selection

Material selection prioritized strength, cost, and compatibility with high-volume manufacturing. Using Granta EduPack, several candidate materials were evaluated, leading to the selection of gray cast iron for its balance of mechanical strength, low cost, and excellent castability.

Phase 3: Design Challenges and Solutions

Extremely high PV values at the vane tips required the use of hardened tool steel. A revised vane geometry was developed to balance internal pressures while maintaining sealing performance. Steel-on-steel wear was mitigated using precision-machined PEEK inserts, significantly extending service life. Inlet and outlet port geometry was also optimized to minimize cavitation risk.

Phase 4: Design for Manufacturing

The housing was designed for sand casting with a single-core configuration to minimize tooling complexity. PEEK inserts were optimized for injection molding, and vanes were designed for powder metal sintering to ensure dimensional accuracy at high production volumes.

Phase 5: Technical Documentation

A complete manufacturing-ready drawing package was produced, including detailed part drawings with GD&T, casting drawings, and injection molding documentation. These deliverables support immediate production implementation.
