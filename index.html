About Me

I am a graduate student pursuing a Master's degree in Mechanical Engineering at Boston University, where I focus on applying innovative solutions to complex engineering challenges. My approach combines technical rigor with creative problem-solving, developed through an unconventional path into engineering.

My academic journey began with a liberal arts degree emphasizing the history of science and philosophy. During my studies, I developed a deep interest in applied mathematics, which led me to pursue engineering through Boston University's LEAP program. This interdisciplinary background provides me with a unique perspective that allows me to approach problems from angles that traditional engineering paths might overlook.

I thrive on challenging projects that push me to learn new skills and develop efficient solutions. My attention to detail and rapid learning ability enable me to quickly adapt to new technologies and methodologies. Whether working on automated systems, mechanical linkages, or design projects, I bring both analytical thinking and creative problem-solving to every challenge.

Projects

Course Projects - ME 360

Project-based course focusing on mechanical design, automation, and motion analysis

Automatic Pipetter for PCR Wells

Automation • Precision Mechanics • Laboratory Equipment

Designed and fabricated an automated pipetting system capable of accurately dispensing liquid into a standard 96-well PCR plate. The project required precise motion control, mechanical design for repeatable positioning, and integration of fluid handling components. Successfully achieved consistent dispensing accuracy across all wells while maintaining contamination-free operation.

Dynamic Beam Balance Car

Control Systems • Mechanical Design • Dynamics

Engineered a motorized vehicle capable of traveling a predetermined distance while maintaining dynamic balance of a one-foot beam. The project integrated motor control, weight distribution analysis, and real-time stabilization to achieve maximum acceleration without beam toppling. Required careful consideration of center of mass, acceleration profiles, and mechanical stability.

Four-Bar Linkage Motion Analysis

SolidWorks • Kinematics • Motion Simulation

Conducted comprehensive motion analysis of a four-bar linkage mechanism using SolidWorks simulation tools. Analyzed velocity profiles, acceleration patterns, and mechanical advantage throughout the complete motion cycle. Documented findings and design recommendations for optimizing linkage performance in various applications.

Automated Trash Can Lid Mechanism

Mechanism Design • CAD Modeling • Functional Prototyping

Developed a four-bar linkage mechanism to automate trash can lid operation. The design focused on smooth motion profiles, appropriate mechanical advantage, and reliable operation through thousands of cycles. Optimized link lengths and pivot positions to achieve the desired lid trajectory while minimizing actuation force requirements.

Personal Projects

Bagatelle Arcade Game

Mechanical Design • Fabrication • Game Design

Designed and fabricated a miniaturized pinball-style Bagatelle game from scratch. Four-Cylinder Engine Simulation

CAD Modeling • Mechanical Simulation • Engine Design

Created a detailed CAD model and motion simulation of a four-cylinder internal combustion engine in SolidWorks. Modeled all major components including pistons, connecting rods, crankshaft, and valvetrain. Simulated complete engine cycles to analyze piston motion, valve timing, and mechanical interactions throughout the operating range.

Electronics • Soldering • Programming

Designed and assembled an electronic device that displays a custom message through timed LED sequences. Involved circuit design, component selection, precise soldering of surface-mount and through-hole components, and programming the display sequence. The compact design required careful attention to component placement and wire routing within size constraints.
